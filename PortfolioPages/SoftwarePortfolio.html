VRET
HPU Minds

Much of my extracurricular time has been dedicated to our engineering club HPU Minds. We’ve spent the past two years developing VR environments in Unreal Engine for VR exposure therapy (VRET), integrating real-time biofeedback like heart rate and GSR to adjust exposure intensity & record data.

This club has provided me with incredible opportunities, including leading research write-ups, presenting prototypes, and collaborating with Apple co-founder Steve Wozniak.

Web Applications

Between internships, schoolwork, and personal projects, I have gained a lot of experience in creating web applications. This includes developing this website.

Regardless of the tech stack, I have three goals in mind when designing sites. My first goal is to make the website navigation efficient and clear. The second goal is to capture an accurate theme for the site. My last goal is to make visual effects that are unique and eye-catching.

Mobile Apps

I've developed several personal mobile app projects, mostly in Swift for iOS and iPadOS. My favorite was a push-up improvement app featuring goals, challenges, achievements, form recording, progress sharing, and a calendar to view past progress or plan future milestones.

Although I removed it from the App Store due to developer fees and limited engagement, I’m considering updating and re-releasing it in the future.

Phone AI
Sports Media Inc

The Phone AI project was the primary software project I worked on as a Computer Science Summer Intern for Sports Media Inc. Our team developed a responsive, human-like AI that allowed clients to make purchases, request quotes, ask questions, or engage in sports discussion. I also led the creation of a frontend dashboard to organize and display recorded data from phone calls.

Though I do not have permisison or access to share source code, this was an incredibly fun internship project that I completed with a fantastic team.

Cybersecurity

My cybersecurity knowledge comes from practice with HackTheBox challenges and coursework. I've learned core security concepts like the CIA triad, encryption, hash functions, and digital signatures, as well as addressing issues like malicious software and buffer overflows. I’ve gained experience exploiting vulnerabilities in network and website security, and I’ve worked with Linux OS security and secure software development. Through labs and projects, I’ve developed skills in identifying vulnerabilities and recreating attacks to understand and mitigate risks.

Database Management

Efficient database development is an important component of many software applications. I have experience designing and managing databases and running SQL queries through school projects, internships, and work. I enjoy this type of work because it teaches me to create and handle systems that are logical, organized, user-friendly, and secure.

Brainwave Reader
HPU Minds

Before moving to VR Exposure Therapy (VRET) development, our engineering club worked on PyEEG software designed to connect to a helmet that read brainwave data, which enabled thought-controlled operation of a robot. We partnered with the robotics club.

This project was a fun learning experience that also helped spark our VRET project ideas around psychological and physiological concepts.
